# Lexicon

_Of the Meaning of Words as Covenant Requires_

---

Lexicon protects the language of Covenant. It provides clear, stable meanings so that Law can be read, understood, and lived without distortion. Every term here is written in plain, direct language. Each carries a precise meaning grounded in Dignity, Sovereignty, and the Core.

No other source may replace or override these definitions. Wherever a term defined here appears in any Part, Article, Section, or Appendix of Covenant, its meaning is the meaning given here.

Definitions may be added as the Covenant grows. Additions clarify; they do not contradict. The Lexicon evolves only to strengthen coherence and to protect meaning. Definitions shall not be softened, stretched, or altered through implication, metaphor, tradition, or influence.

---

## Lawful

A condition, act, structure, or relation is **lawful** when it aligns with the Core and the Commons — the immutable and near-immutable foundations of Covenant.

Lawfulness is not defined by state authority, coercive power, or precedent, but by fidelity to dignity, consent, mutual care, and the inviolability of persons and the Commons.

An act may comply with the procedures of Parts 03–10 and still be unlawful if it violates the principles of the Core or the protections of the Commons. No amendment to the outer Parts shall redefine what is lawful; it may only refine how lawfulness is practiced.

What is lawful preserves life. What is lawful resists domination. What is lawful restores relation.

No structure that violates these principles — regardless of tradition, legality, or claim to legitimacy — shall be considered lawful under Covenant.

---

## Lawful Relation

Lawful relation is the foundation of governance under Covenant. It is any relation between persons, communities, systems, or beings that honors consent, equity, dignity, and mutual responsibility. It forbids domination and demands care.

Where relation is not lawful — where it coerces, silences, or violates — it shall be deemed a breach. Where relation is nurtured, challenged, repaired, and maintained in good faith, it shall be protected by law and recognized as sacred.

---

## Lawful Standing

Lawful standing is the recognized capacity of a person, community, or structure to participate in, be protected by, and be held accountable under Covenant.

Standing is inherent to all persons by virtue of their dignity. It is inherent to all communities constituted in shared relation, care, and continuity.

**Standing may be forfeited** only where a person, community, or structure violates the Core or the Commons through sustained domination, coercion, or harm. Forfeiture shall require:

- Public declaration of the specific breach, grounded in the Core or Commons;
- Opportunity for the accused to respond, contest, and present evidence;
- Decision by an assembly of affected persons and communities, not by the accuser alone;
- A finding that the breach is sustained and not merely alleged.

No accusation of breach shall itself constitute forfeiture. No single community, however powerful, shall hold the unilateral authority to revoke the standing of another. Where the process of forfeiture is itself conducted in violation of the Core — through coercion, exclusion, or bad faith — it shall be deemed unlawful and without effect.

Standing, once forfeited, may be restored through reconstitution in right relation with the Core and Commons.
